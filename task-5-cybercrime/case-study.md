UPI fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in which people are cheated by fraudulent means. The common scenario in UPI fraud is as follows:

A person who is committing the cybercrime contacts the victim and pretends to be a bank employee, customer care executive, or buyer in an online transaction. The person who is committing the cybercrime sends a payment request or link to the victim and asks him or her to approve it. The person who is committing the cybercrime tells the victim that he or she will be able to receive money by approving that request. However, instead of receiving money, he or she loses money from his or her account.

Such cybercrime is commonly committed on students who are not aware of the actual functioning of digital payment systems. The consequences that are faced by people who are cheated in UPI fraud include financial loss and mental stress in retrieving the lost money.

This type of cybercrime is an indication that people need to be cautious while making transactions through online payment systems.
